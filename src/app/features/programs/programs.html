GNM

General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) is a 3 to 3.5-year diploma program focusing on practical clinical care, midwifery, and community health. It trains students for roles as Registered Nurse Registered Midwives (RNRM) in hospitals, clinics, and community centers. The curriculum covers nursing foundations, anatomy, pharmacology, and maternal health.

PB.B.Sc. Nursing

Post Basic B.Sc. degree in Nursing is a broad based education aimed to build upon the skills and competencies acquired at the diploma in nursing level. It is specifically directed to the upgrading of critical thinking skills, competencies and standards required for practice of professional nursing and midwifery as envisaged in National Health Policy.

B.Sc. MIT

A Bachelor of Science in Medical Imaging Technology (B.Sc. MIT) is an undergraduate program training professionals to operate diagnostic equipment like X-rays, CT scans, MRIs, and ultrasound. It focuses on anatomy, physiology, radiation safety, and patient care, preparing students for roles as radiology technologists in hospitals and clinics.

B.Sc. Optometry

B.Sc. Optometry is a 4-year undergraduate program (typically 3 years of study + 1-year internship) focused on eye care, vision science, and diagnosing ocular disorders. It prepares students to become primary healthcare professionals skilled in prescribing glasses, contact lenses, and managing eye diseases.
